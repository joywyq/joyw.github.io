About


Hey there! I'm Joy, and I'm really happy that you're here reading this.

I'm a Singaporean, and I've been living in all its sweltering heat for the past 20 years. In my middle school and high school years I went to School of the Arts, Singapore, which taught the International Baccalaureate program but added on a whole lot of culture and perspective in terms of art-making.

Now I'm a penultimate year student studying Psychology and Linguistics at the University of Oxford, and also the designer for the Oxford Artificial Intelligence Society (they're are a really awesome bunch of people - not robots). The part I love the most about being here is that I can surround myself with people who are deeply passionate in their fields and from whom I can learn. I'm very much interested in working in a cognitive science-related field in the future, whether that's in the form of research or UX design.

I'm the kind of person who likes reading up about something cool online, throwing myself at a project or community to be a part of it, realising that it's more work than expected, and trying to do them anyway. Thankfully, its led to many really great results. Over the last summer I was doing an internship at Singapore's Agency for Science, Technology and Research (A*STAR) where I was brought onboard as a user researcher, which quickly turned into a UX design project, and I hadn't even heard of UX beforehand. Before I entered university I was an English tuition teacher, a cook at a French restaurant, and a social worker/research assistant. I've been trying to learn how to code, and evidently from this website I'm not very good yet. But I'm trying!

I'm always up for a conversation. Get in touch with me at joywyq18@gmail.com and I will talk to you about psychometrics, typography, neural networks, arts education, recipes, film, good science, Queen, and anything you're passionate about.
